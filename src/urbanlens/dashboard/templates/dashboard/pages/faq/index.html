Getting started

The basics.

What is UrbanLens? expand_more

UrbanLens is a private, purpose-built mapping platform for urban explorers, historians, and photographers who want to document forgotten places responsibly. Drop pins, keep notes, share selectively, and coordinate trips - all without broadcasting sensitive locations to the world. Read more on our About page.

What's the difference between a Pin and a Location? expand_more

A Location is the shared, canonical record of a physical place - its coordinates, address, and name. A Pin is your personal record of that place: your own notes, custom name, priority, status, and visit history. Many users can pin the same Location without seeing each other's notes, since Pins are private by default.

Is UrbanLens free to use? expand_more

Yes. UrbanLens is free, open source, and not funded by ads or data sales. You can check out the source code on our GitHub page.

Do I need an account to use the map? expand_more

You can browse public content without one, but you'll need a free account to drop pins, write wiki entries, plan trips, or use safety check-ins. Create an account or sign in to get started.

I already have pins saved elsewhere. Can I bring them in? expand_more

Yes - you can import pins from a CSV file. Every imported pin is shown to you for review before anything is saved, and imports are never made public automatically. See the pin import guide for details.

{# -- Privacy & sharing -------------------------------------------------------- #}
Privacy & sharing

Who can see what.

Are my pins public by default? expand_more

No. Every pin is private by default. Sharing is opt-in and granular - you choose whether to share a pin with specific friends, a trip, or no one at all.

What's a wiki, and is it separate from my pin? expand_more

A location's wiki is a community-editable page for history, architectural notes, hazards, and access details. It's entirely separate from your personal pin - contributing to a wiki never exposes your pin's notes, coordinates, or visit history, and participation is always opt-in.

Can other users find me or my pins without my permission? expand_more

No. Other users can't discover you until you take an action to become visible to them. Even friend requests don't reveal whether an account exists until the request is accepted. Read more on our Values page.

Do you sell location data or run ads? expand_more

Never. We don't sell pin, photo, or location data, and we don't run ads. There's nothing on UrbanLens whose purpose is to monetize what you document.

{# -- Features ---------------------------------------------------------------- #}
Features

Maps, trips, and photos.

Can I plan group trips without revealing exact locations? expand_more

Yes. Trips support RSVPs, member management, and shared itineraries with per-trip privacy controls, so you can coordinate an expedition without exposing pin details to anyone who hasn't been invited.

Can UrbanLens create pins automatically from my photos? expand_more

If you opt in, UrbanLens can read GPS coordinates from your photos' EXIF metadata and suggest a matching pin - or let you create one directly. Photos without GPS data can be dragged onto the map to assign a location manually.

Is there a limit to how much I can upload? expand_more

Accounts have a storage quota for photos and attachments, shown on your profile page. Uploaded images are automatically optimized to help you fit more within your quota without losing meaningful detail.

Where do the extra details on a pin's page come from (Wikipedia, weather, etc.)? expand_more

Pin detail pages can pull in supplementary context from external sources - things like Wikipedia summaries, historical imagery, weather, and relevant public datasets - to help you research a location before you visit.

{# -- Safety -------------------------------------------------------------------- #}
Safety

Staying safe in the field.

What is a safety check-in? expand_more

A safety check-in lets you tell trusted contacts where you're going and when to expect you back. If you don't check in by your expected return time, it can automatically escalate and notify the people you've chosen.

Can a safety check-in notify people who aren't on UrbanLens? expand_more

Yes. Safety check-ins support notifying contacts by email in addition to other UrbanLens users, so the people who need to know you're overdue don't need an account of their own.

Can I attach hazard notes to a pin? expand_more

Yes. Pins support structural hazard notes and vulnerability ratings, so you and anyone you share the pin with can see known risks before heading out.

{# -- Account & data -------------------------------------------------------------- #}
Account & data

Your data, your rules.

Can I see everything UrbanLens stores about me? expand_more

Yes. Any data we store about you is visible on your own profile page - there's no hidden user data on UrbanLens that isn't displayed back to you.

Can I delete my account and data? expand_more

Yes. Account deletion is available from your profile settings and removes your personal data from UrbanLens.

Is UrbanLens open source? Can I contribute? expand_more

Yes - the full source is on GitHub. Contributions are welcome, and we ask that any contributed code align with the principles on our Values page.

Who built UrbanLens? expand_more

UrbanLens is built and maintained by one person, not a company. It started as a personal tool for a small circle of friends and grew from there. Read the full story on our About page.
